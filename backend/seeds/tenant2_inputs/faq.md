# Frequently Asked Questions

## Are you taking new patients?

Yes. We have capacity for new patients on all three of our chairs. The first
visit is a new patient examination, which is a longer appointment than a
routine check-up because we chart every tooth and take baseline X-rays.

## Do I have to have X-rays at my first visit?

We recommend them, because without a baseline we are diagnosing from the
visible surfaces only and decay between teeth is invisible to the eye. You
can decline, and we will note that on your record and work from what we can
see. If you have had X-rays taken elsewhere within the last two years, we
can request them from your previous practice instead.

## Does it hurt?

Any treatment beyond a clean is done under local anaesthetic, so the
procedure itself should not hurt. Most patients find the anaesthetic
injection itself the least comfortable part, and we use a numbing gel on the
gum first to take the edge off that. Tell us if you are anxious - we would
much rather go slowly than have you dread coming back.

## I am very nervous about the dentist. What can you do?

Tell us when you book and we will schedule a longer, quieter appointment and
spend the first part of it just talking, with no instruments. We can agree a
stop signal before we start. We do not offer sedation on site; patients who
need sedation are referred to a specialist practice we work with, and we
will make that referral for you.

## Do you treat children?

Yes, from their first tooth. Under-16s need a parent or guardian present for
the whole appointment. Bringing a young child along to your own check-up
first, just to sit and watch, is a genuinely good way to make their own
first visit uneventful.

## How often should I come in?

Every six months for most people. Patients with active gum disease, a high
decay rate, or certain medical conditions are usually put on a three or four
month recall instead, and we will tell you clearly if that applies to you
and why.

## My tooth stopped hurting. Do I still need to come in?

Almost certainly yes. Pain that stops on its own often means the nerve
inside the tooth has died rather than that the problem resolved, and the
infection continues silently. Please still get it looked at.

## Do you do teeth whitening?

Yes, both take-home trays and a single-visit in-chair treatment. Whitening
only works on natural tooth structure - it does not change the colour of
crowns, veneers, or fillings, so if you have visible restorations at the
front we will talk through what the result will actually look like before
you commit.

## Can I get a filling the same day as my check-up?

Sometimes, if the appointment book allows and the filling is straightforward.
Ask when you book and we will allow extra time rather than have you come
back a second time.

## Do you take my insurance?

We submit claims electronically for the plans we are in-network with, and
for every other plan we give you a fully itemised receipt with procedure
codes so you can claim it back yourself. Ask the front desk about your
specific plan - we will tell you honestly which of the two applies.

## What if I cannot afford the treatment I need?

Tell us. There is very often a staged approach: stabilise the urgent
problems first, plan the rest over time. For treatment plans over one
thousand dollars we offer an interest-free six-month in-house payment plan.
We would far rather build a plan you can actually afford than have you
avoid treatment entirely.

## Do you offer parking?

There are six spaces behind the building, accessed from the side street, and
free two-hour street parking on the main road. The practice is on the ground
floor with step-free access from the rear car park.
