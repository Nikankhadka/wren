# Practice Policies

## Appointments and Scheduling

New patients are asked to arrive fifteen minutes before their first
appointment so we have time to complete a medical history form and take
baseline X-rays. Returning patients only need to arrive five minutes early.
We hold appointment slots for ten minutes past the scheduled time; after
that we may need to shorten the visit or reschedule, because running late
on one chair pushes every patient behind it back.

Routine check-ups are booked on a six-month recall. We send a reminder by
text one week ahead and again the morning of the appointment. If you prefer
email or a phone call instead, tell the front desk and we will change it on
your record.

## Cancellations and Missed Appointments

We ask for at least twenty-four hours' notice to cancel or move an
appointment. Cancelling with less notice, or not showing up at all, is
recorded as a missed appointment. The first missed appointment in a
twelve-month period carries no charge. After that we charge a fifty dollar
missed-appointment fee, which covers the chair time we could not offer to
another patient. The fee is waived for genuine emergencies, illness, and
anything else you tell us about honestly - we would much rather know than
guess.

## Payment and Insurance

Payment is due at the time of treatment. We accept cash, debit, and all
major credit cards. We are not an insurer and we do not bill insurers
directly for every plan, but we do submit claims electronically on your
behalf for the plans we are in-network with, and for out-of-network plans
we give you a fully itemised receipt with procedure codes so you can claim
it yourself.

We will always give you a written treatment estimate before starting any
work beyond a routine check-up and clean, so there is never a surprise at
the desk. An estimate is what we expect the treatment to cost; if we find
something unexpected once we are working, we stop and talk to you before
continuing rather than adding to the bill silently.

## Payment Plans

For treatment plans over one thousand dollars we offer an interest-free
in-house payment plan spread over six months, with the first instalment due
on the day of treatment. There is no credit check and no administration
fee. Ask at the front desk before treatment begins - we cannot move an
already-completed treatment onto a plan retroactively.

## Emergencies

We keep two emergency slots open every weekday morning for existing
patients in pain. Call as early in the day as you can. Outside opening
hours, our answering service will page the on-call dentist for genuine
dental emergencies: uncontrolled bleeding, significant swelling that is
spreading, a knocked-out permanent tooth, or trauma to the face and jaw. A
knocked-out permanent tooth is time-critical - keep it in milk or saliva,
never scrub it, and get to us within the hour if you possibly can.

## Records and Privacy

Your dental records, including images, are kept for a minimum of ten years
after your last visit and are never shared with anyone outside the practice
without your written consent, except where the law requires it. You can
request a copy of your own records at any time; we provide them within
thirty days at no charge for the first request in a calendar year.

## Children

We see children from their first tooth. Under-16s must be accompanied by a
parent or guardian for the whole appointment. Check-ups and fluoride
treatments for children under 16 who are registered with the practice are
included in the family plan; see the fee sheet for what that covers.
